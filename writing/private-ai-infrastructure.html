Why your personal AI should be private by default

March 2026 · Infrastructure

I've been building a setup where my AI assistant has its own private corner of the internet.

Here's how it works: a $5 Lightsail instance runs Nginx with mTLS — mutual TLS, meaning only devices with the right certificate can even see the page. My phone has a certificate. Random internet crawlers don't. The AI lives behind that.

Then there's a /public/ folder — intentionally open, no auth. That's where things like email signatures and shared assets live. Everything else? Locked down.

The interesting part isn't the tech stack — it's the design decision. You're building an AI system that handles personal context, manages files, sends emails on your behalf. That should be private by default, with deliberate exceptions. Not public by default with locks slapped on.

Most "personal AI" setups I see are the opposite. They're hosted somewhere public, maybe behind a password. That's not good enough.

mTLS is the right primitive here. Worth the 20 minutes of setup.
