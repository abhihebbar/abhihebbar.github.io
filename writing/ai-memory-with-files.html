AI memory doesn't need a database. Just files.

March 2026 · AI

The thing nobody talks about with personal AI is memory.

Every session, the model wakes up fresh. No idea who you are, what you care about, what happened yesterday. You either re-explain everything every time, or you build a memory system.

I built one. It's just files.

  • MEMORY.md — long-term. Distilled facts about me, my preferences, how I work. The AI reads this at the start of every session.
  • memory/YYYY-MM-DD.md — daily logs. Raw notes from each session. What happened, what was decided, what to follow up on.
  • SOUL.md — who the AI is. Personality, working style, how it should behave. This one it can edit itself when it learns something.
  • FOLLOWUPS.md — things I need to do. The AI runs a heartbeat check periodically, reads this file, nudges me if something needs attention.

The whole system is maybe 10 files. But because it's just markdown on a server, it's persistent across sessions, editable by both me and the AI, and under my control completely.

No vendor lock-in. No "memory" feature that disappears when you change models. Just files.

The simplest solutions are usually the right ones.
